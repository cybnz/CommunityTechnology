Tiles IoT Inventor Toolkit

Group A

Challenge

Our community feel it’s important council prioritise nature. How might technology help protect, preserve, and enhance our city centre’s natural environment?

Summary

A centralised app and information totems that connects Tauranga residents with their environment, focusing on the waterfront as a key community space while expanding to cover broader city activities and information. The app provides a singular source of information, and the totems are a live feed and display of data, also shared into the app. The app and connected totems would make public transport easy and accessible and make environmental info accessible to raise awareness.

Key Points

  • Connected totems:
    • Solar-powered information totems and buoys positioned along the wharf featuring:
      • Real-time water quality measurements (temperature, safety levels)
      • UV index and weather conditions
      • Digital displays showing:
        • Instant replay footage of manu jumps
        • Upcoming city events
        • Important community notices
    • Automated safety barriers that activate when water conditions become unsafe, also shared to the app
    • Secure phone lockers for water activities
    • Camera system specifically configured for:
      • Capturing manu jumps for instant replay
      • Potential live streaming during competitions
      • Future expansion to other waterfront activities
  • Centralised app that serves as a one-stop hub for Tauranga residents and visitors:
    • Environmental information:
      • Direct feed from waterfront sensors
      • Water safety status
      • Weather conditions and forecasts
      • Environmental alerts and updates
    • Transportation:
      • Complete public transport information for all Tauranga routes
      • Real-time bus tracking
      • Trip planning tools
      • Transport carbon footprint information
    • Community features:
      • City-wide events calendar
      • Live camera feeds
      • Community notices and updates
      • Manu competition scores and replays

Group B

Challenge

Tauranga’s beautiful landscape and coastal location expose us to natural hazards. As temperatures rise, we’ll face more extreme and unpredictable weather events. How might technology provide early warning in the city centre for natural hazards?

Summary

A city-wide emergency communication system that transforms existing CBD infrastructure, such as digital displays, into an emergency broadcast network during natural disasters, like coordinating tsunami response, redirecting public transport to assist evacuation.

Key Points

  • Emergency display network:
    • Co-opted digital infrastructure including:
      • Commercial advertising displays
      • Information kiosks
      • Public address systems
      • Electronic notice boards
    • During normal operations:
      • Displays continue regular content (advertising, events, city information)
      • Periodic display of evacuation route reminders, and emergency preparedness information
    • During emergencies:
      • Immediate switch to emergency mode
      • Clear, visible evacuation instructions
      • Communications for those without devices
      • Audio and visual to help those with impairments
      • Real-time hazard information
      • Safe route guidance using directional indicators
      • Can identify citizens left behind
  • Dynamic transport reallocation:
    • Emergency bus route system:
      • Existing buses redirected from routes to pick up people from signage points
      • Pre-planned evacuation routes for best/nearest safe zone
      • Real-time route adjustment based on hazard conditions
      • Temporary bus stops at key gathering points
      • Integration with existing bus fleet management
      • Priority corridors for emergency transport
  • Central emergency control system:
    • Single-switch activation for all display networks
    • Integration with Civil Defence alerts
    • Automated message templates for different scenarios
    • Priority focus on tsunami warnings and response
    • Capability to handle multiple hazard types:
      • Severe weather events
      • Flooding
      • Earthquakes
      • Serious accidents

Group C

Challenge

At night, less than three in ten residents feel safe in the city centre. How might we use technology to improve safety in our CBD?

Summary

An intelligent urban lighting system that combines decorative elements with safety features, using subtle lighting and planting to guide pedestrian behaviour and discourage antisocial activity in Tauranga's CBD at night.

Key Points

  • Decorative safety lighting:
    • Integrated lighting in existing planter boxes and new strategic locations
    • Subtle colour changes and lighting patterns that:
      • Create welcoming, well-lit pathways
      • Highlight safe walking routes
      • Respond to pedestrian movement
      • Enhance the natural elements of the CBD
  • AI-assisted monitoring:
    • Discrete camera system that triggers gentle lighting changes when detecting:
      • Loitering
      • Graffiti attempts
      • Intimidating behaviour
    • Responses focus on positive guidance rather than alerts:
      • Brightening alternative pathways
      • Creating more welcoming spaces through lighting
      • Subtly discouraging problem areas through reduced illumination
      • Sound cues to provide awareness of social expectations
  • Environmental integration:
    • Smart lighting designed to complement existing CBD features:
      • Enhanced planter box lighting on one-way streets
      • Coordinated lighting patterns between zones
      • Emphasis on creating beautiful, safe-feeling spaces
      • Integration with natural elements to enhance the urban environment

Futurice IoT Service Kit

Group A

Challenge

When we asked people what they would change about Tauranga, the top response was to reduce traffic congestion. How might technology make it easy for people to shift to cycling, walking, car sharing, or riding scooters or buses when visiting the city centre?

Summary

A connected public transport system that prioritises safe student travel while encouraging broader community use through smart tracking, convenient facilities, and engaging incentives.

Key Points

  • Smart wristband tracking:
    • Tracks journey progress via route sensors
    • Acts as payment method for transport
    • Sends automated notifications to parents at key points:
      • Boarding bus
      • Arriving at destination
      • Entering bike lane networks
    • Privacy-focused design for student protection
  • Enhanced public transport management:
    • Route optimisation based on usage patterns:
      • Real-time capacity monitoring
      • Adjustment of bus sizes based on demand
      • Integration with school bus routes
      • Peak time capacity management
    • Focus on regular route enhancement rather than new routes
  • CBD end-of-journey facilities:
    • Secure bike storage
    • Free Wi-Fi access
    • Device charging stations
    • Weather protection
    • Easy connection to bus routes
  • Gamified transport usage tracking:
    • Personal leaderboards showing:
      • Number of trips taken
      • Estimated emissions saved
      • Journey time improvements
      • Alternative transport usage
    • Potential rates reduction rewards
    • Community challenge events (Wednesday Challenge)
    • School participation programs

Group B

Challenge

Our city centre has a rich and diverse history, from its Māori heritage, through to European settlement and beyond. How might technology tell the stories of our city centre?

Summary

An interactive art trail through Tauranga's CBD that brings history to life through a mix of physical and digital installations, weaving together cultural narratives, historical collections, and new artworks in collaboration with local iwi.

Key Points

  • Multi-modal art experiences:
    • Interactive installations responding to:
      • Pedestrian movement and presence
      • Weather conditions
      • Time of day
      • Seasonal changes
    • Variety of art forms:
      • Traditional physical artworks
      • Light installations
      • Water features
      • Kinetic sculptures
      • Digital/virtual overlays
      • Sound elements
  • Check-in kiosks:
    • Interactive checkpoints that:
      • Guide visitors between installations
      • Provide historical context
      • Share cultural stories
      • Track visitor engagement
      • Enable social sharing
    • Integration with mobile devices for:
      • Extended information access
      • AR experiences
      • Navigation assistance
      • Optional tour tracking
  • Content curation:
    • Partnership with local iwi for narrative direction
    • Space for both traditional and contemporary stories
    • Platform for local artists
    • Integration of archived collections:
      • Art gallery pieces
      • Library collections
      • Historical archives
      • New commissioned works
  • Smart infrastructure:
    • Environmental sensors
    • Interactive touch points
    • Lighting controls
    • Mobile integration
    • Visitor analytics
    • Navigation aids

Group C

Challenge

When we asked people what they would change about Tauranga, the top response was to reduce traffic congestion. How might technology make it easy for people to shift to cycling, walking, car sharing, or riding scooters or buses when visiting the city centre?

Summary

An integrated system that prioritises sustainable transport options through connected infrastructure, smart facilities, and real-time journey optimisation, making car-free travel the most attractive option for accessing Tauranga's CBD.

Key Points

  • Smart street infrastructure:
    • Solar-powered smart benches featuring:
      • Device charging points
      • Free Wi-Fi access
      • Secure bike locks
      • Real-time transport information displays
      • Integration with transport app
    • Priority traffic signals for:
      • Buses
      • Cyclists
      • Pedestrian crossings
  • End-of-journey facilities:
    • Secure bike storage
    • Shower facilities
    • Changing rooms
    • Personal lockers
    • Toilets
    • Device charging stations
    • Bike repair stations
  • Connected transport app and displays:
    • Real-time journey planning showing:
      • Comparative travel times by mode
      • Available bike rentals
      • Ferry departure times
      • Bus locations
      • Walking routes
    • Live updates on:
      • Facility availability
      • Traffic conditions
      • Journey time estimates
      • Best route recommendations
  • Transport integration support:
    • Infrastructure to support:
      • Bike rental stations
      • Ferry service connections (if available)
      • Bus priority lanes
      • Real-time journey optimisation
    • Focus on making sustainable transport faster than private vehicles
