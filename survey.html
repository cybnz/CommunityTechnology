Survey Results Summary

The Community Technology Survey requested views on where community technology can benefit council services and city liveability. The survey was distributed through the Let's Talk Tauranga newsletter, of which most subscribers are ratepayers. 248 responses were received, of which a similar number were from males and females. 44% of the responses were from those aged 65+, with a roughly even distribution of ages across all other brackets.

Respondents would most like to see more technology around environmental monitoring and emergency response systems, digital community messaging and signage in outdoor spaces, free public Wi-Fi, and interactive learning and technology for kids, in that order. Respondents believed it is important that technology makes it easier to move around the city and use sustainable transport choices, helping protect and improve the environment and make council operations more sustainable. Respondents wanted public safety, traffic congestion, and reliable and timely public transport most improved; specifically for council services, roads, footpaths, and cycleways, followed by drinking water, wastewater, and stormwater, then rubbish and recycling being most important to improve with technology.

Most respondents were unaware of the current council-provided community technology, with only free Wi-Fi at city libraries, and digital signs for bus arrivals and parking availability having many people being aware of them. While most respondents didn't report significant obstacles to technology use, with privacy and security, and already having the technology they required being the primary inhibitors. 23% of respondents rated their digital skills as being neutral through to very low, with the vast majority feeling confident in using community technology. Many open-text fields were utilised to provide unrelated feedback on “wasteful spending”. There are variations in the responses regarding the importance of issues due to the phrasing of the question and issue, such as the importance of safety when included with phrasing on inclusivity.

From these responses, the following key challenges that technology could help to improve were identified: transport, environment, safety, and communication. These challenges informed focus areas for the workshop conducted on the 18th of December.
